**Key editorial decisions documented:**

| Decision | Rationale |
|---|---|
| `diacritics_status: "added"` | The original text carries no tashkeel; diacritics supplied in full from scratch |
| `قَدَّسَ` / `طَهَّرَ` (Form II verbs) | Both read as *faʿʿala* (intensive/causative) — قَدَسَ Form I would mean "to be holy," whereas قَدَّسَ means "to sanctify (someone)"; likewise طَهَّرَ means "to purify (someone)" |
| `بْنُ` (rafa') / `بْنِ` (jarr) | First *ibn* is nominative (predicate of the subject chain); second is genitive (governed by the first *ibn*'s idāfa) |
| `رَمْسَهُ` | Classical term for the covered grave/tomb, distinct from the more common قَبْر; retained and glossed in all key_terms |
| Al-Mufid role tagged `"imam"` | He is the *subject* of praise, not a transmitter; called الإمام as an honorific for a senior jurist — the closest available role enum |
| `الشَّهِيدُ الثَّانِي` role tagged `"author"` | He is the *source* of the written eulogy, not a link in a transmission chain |
