English reference translation: Abu Ja’far Muhammad Bin Yaqoub informed us saying, ‘Ali Bin Ibrahim Bin Hashim narrated to me, from his father, from Al Hassan Bin Ibrahim, from Yunus Bin Abdul Rahman, from Ali Bin Mansour who said, ‘Hisham Bin Al Hakam said to me, 
‘There was an atheist in Egypt. Certain things about Abu Abdullah<sup style="font-weight: normal">asws</sup> reached him. So he went out to Al-Medina in order to debate with him<sup style="font-weight: normal">asws</sup>. So he came across us while we were with Abu Abdullah<sup style="font-weight: normal">asws</sup> during the <span class="iTxt">Tawaaf</span>; and it was so that his name was Abdul Malik (Slave of the king) and his teknonym was Abu Abdullah (Father of the slave of Allah<sup style="font-weight: normal">azwj</sup>). 
So he struck his own shoulder with the shoulder of Abu Abdullah<sup style="font-weight: normal">asws</sup>. So Abu Abdullah<sup style="font-weight: normal">asws</sup> said to him: ‘What is your name?’ So he said, ‘My name is Abdul Malik (Slave of the king)’. He<sup style="font-weight: normal">asws</sup> said: ‘So what is your teknonym?’ He said, ‘My teknonym is Abu Abdullah (Father of the slave of Allah<sup style="font-weight: normal">azwj</sup>)’. So Abu Abdullah<sup style="font-weight: normal">asws</sup> said to him: ‘So who is this king whose slave you are? Is he from the kings of the earth or from the kings of the sky? And, inform me<sup style="font-weight: normal">asws</sup> about your son, is he a slave of a god of the sky, or a slave of a god of the earth? Say whatever you like, it would be against you’.  
Hisham Bin Al-Hakam said, ‘So I said to the atheist, ‘You are not replying to him<sup style="font-weight: normal">asws</sup>?’ So he disliked my words. So Abu Abdullah<sup style="font-weight: normal">asws</sup> said: ‘When we are free from the <span class="iTxt">Tawaaf</span>, then come over to us’. So when Abu Abdullah<sup style="font-weight: normal">asws</sup> was free, the atheist came over to him<sup style="font-weight: normal">asws</sup>, and he sat himself in front of Abu Abdullah<sup style="font-weight: normal">asws</sup>, and we gathered in his<sup style="font-weight: normal">asws</sup> presence. 
So Abu Abdullah<sup style="font-weight: normal">asws</sup> said to the atheist: ‘Do you know that for the earth there is an underneath and an above?’ He said, ‘Yes’. He<sup style="font-weight: normal">asws</sup> said: ‘So have you entered its underneath?’ He said, ‘No’. He<sup style="font-weight: normal">asws</sup> said: ‘So what would make you know what is underneath it?’ He said, ‘I don’t know except that I guess that there is nothing underneath it’. So Abu Abdullah<sup style="font-weight: normal">asws</sup> said: ‘So the guess shows the ‘عَجْزٌ’ (inability) due to what you are not certain of’. 
Then Abu Abdullah<sup>asws</sup> said: ‘So have you ascended to the sky?’ He said, ‘No’. He<sup>asws</sup> said: ‘Do you know what is therein?’ He said, ‘No’. He<sup>asws</sup> said: ‘It is strange for you that you have not reached the west and not descended into the earth and not ascended to the sky, and not exceeded over there as to what is behind these, and you are denying with what is in these? And would the intellectual deny what he does not recognise?’ The atheist said, ‘No one has spoken with me by this apart from you<sup>asws</sup>’. So Abu Abdullah<sup>asws</sup> said: ‘So you are in doubt with regards to that. So perhaps He<sup>azwj</sup> is (Existent), and perhaps He<sup>azwj</sup> is not (Existent)’. So the atheist said: ‘And perhaps it is that’. 
So Abu Abdullah<sup>asws</sup> said: ‘O you man! There is no argument for the one who does not know over the one who does know, and there is no argument for the ignorance either. O brother of the people of Egypt! Understand about me<sup>asws</sup>, for we<sup>asws</sup> do not doubt regarding Allah<sup>azwj</sup>, ever! But, do you not see the sun and the moon, and the night and the day following their course, so they do not get confused and do not retract, (but are) being Forced? There is no place for them except for their respective placing. So if they had power upon the going, they would not be returning, and if they were not being Forced, the night would not become a day, and the day, a night. They are being forced. By Allah<sup>azwj</sup>, O brother of the people of Egypt! The One<sup>azwj</sup> Who Forces them to their shifts is more Wiser than both of them and greater’. So the atheist said, ‘You<sup>asws</sup> speak the truth’. 
Then Abu Abu Abdullah<sup>asws</sup> said: ‘O brother of the people of Egypt! That what they (people) are going towards, and you are thinking that it is the time. If it was the time going with them, why is it not returning them, and if it was returning them, why is it not going with them. The people are being Forced.
O brother of the people of Egypt! Why is the sky elevated and the earth is laid? Why doesn’t the sky fall upon the earth? Why doesn’t the earth stoop above its layers, and they are neither attached nor are they attached to the ones above it?’ The atheist said, ‘Allah<sup>azwj</sup> their Lord<sup>azwj</sup> and their Master Keeps them attached’. 
He (the narrator) said, ‘So the atheists believed upon the hands of Abu Abdullah<sup>asws</sup>. So Humran said to him<sup>asws</sup>, ‘May I be sacrificed for you<sup>asws</sup>! If the atheist professed belief upon your<sup>asws</sup> hands, so the disbelievers had professed belief upon the hands of your<sup>asws</sup> father<sup>asws</sup>’. So the Believer who had just professed belief upon the hands of Abu Abdullah<sup>asws</sup> said, ‘Make me to be from your<sup>asws</sup> students’. So Abu Abdullah<sup>asws</sup> said, ‘O Hisham Bin Al-Hakam! Keep him to yourself (company) and teach him’. So Hisham taught him, and he became a teacher of the people of <span class="iTxt">Eman</span> in Syria and the people of Egypt, and his purity was good to the extent that Abu Abdullah<sup>asws</sup> was pleased with him’.
