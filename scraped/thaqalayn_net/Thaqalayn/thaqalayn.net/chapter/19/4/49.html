49. The Apple

Back to book

- عنه، قال: وقال أبو عبد الله (ع): التفاح يفرج المعدة، وقال: كل التفاح فانه يطفئ الحرارة، ويبرد الجوف، ويذهب بالحمى. وفى حديث آخر يذهب بالوباء


1. From him who said, ‘And Abu Abdullah (a.s.) said: ‘The apple relieves the stomach’. And he (a.s.) said: ‘Eat the apple, for it extinguishes the heat, and cools down the interior (stomach), and does away with the fever’. And in another Hadeeth - : ‘Does away with the plague’.

Hadith to report:

1. From him who said, ‘And Abu Abdullah (a.s.) said: ‘The apple relieves the stomach’. And he (a.s.) said: ‘Eat the apple, for it extinguishes the heat, and cools down the interior (stomach), and does away with the fever’. And in another Hadeeth - : ‘Does away with the plague’.


عنه، عن أبى يوسف، عن القندى، عن المفضل بن عمر، عن أبى عبد الله (ع) قال: ذكر له الحمى فقال: إنا أهل بيت لا نتداوى إلى بافاضة الماء البارد يصب علينا، وأكل التفاح.


2. From him, from Abu Yusuf, from Al Qindy, from Al Mufazzal Bin Umar, When the fever was mentioned to Abu Abdullah (a.s.), he (a.s.) said: ‘We (a.s.) the People of the Household (a.s.) do not cure (fever) except by the pouring of the cold water upon us (a.s.), and eating the apple’.

عنه، عن أبيه، عن يونس، عمن ذكره، عن أبى عبد الله (ع) قال: لو يعلم - الناس ما في التفاح، ما داووا مرضاهم إلا به.


3. From him, from his father, from Yunus, from the one who mentioned it, Abu Abdullah (a.s.) has said: ‘If the people knew what was in the apple, they would not cure their sick ones except by it’.

4. From him, from one of them, Abu Abdullah (a.s.) has said: ‘Feed the apples to your feverish ones, for there is none from the things which is more beneficial than the apple’.

عنه، عن محمد بن على الهمداني، عن عبد الله بن سنان، عن درست بن أبى - منصور الواسطي، قال: بعثنى المفضل بن عمر إلى أبى عبد الله (ع) فدخلت عليه في يوم صائف وقدامه طبق فيه تفاح أخضر فو الله إن صبرت أن قلت له: جعلت فداك أتأكل هذا والناس يكرهونه؟ - فقال (ع) كأنه لم يزل يعرفني: إنى وعكت في ليلتى هذه، فبعثت فأتيت به، وهذا يقطع الحمى، ويسكن الحرارة، فقدمت فأصبت أهلى محمومين، فأطعمتهم فأقلعت عنهم.


5. From him, from Muhammad Bin Ali Al Hamdany, from Abdullah Bin Sinan, from Darsat Bin Abu Mansour Al Wasity who said, ‘Al-Mufazzal Bin Umar sent me to Abu Abdullah (a.s.). So I came up to him (a.s.) during a hot summer’s day and in front of him (a.s.) was a platter on which was a green apple, so by Allah (azwj), I could not wait to say to him, ‘May I be sacrificed for you (a.s.)! You (a.s.) are eating this and the people are disliking it?’ So he (a.s.) said: ‘I (a.s.) had slight illness in this night, so I (a.s.) sent for it and was given it, and this cuts off the fever, and settles the heat. Therefore, I (a.s.) proceeded and gave it to the feverish ones of my (a.s.) family. So I (a.s.) fed them and it reduced (the fever) from them’.

6. From him, from Muhammad Bin Jamhour, from Al Hassan Bin Masny, from Suleyman Bin Darastawiya Al Waisty who said, ‘Al-Mufazzal Bin Umar directed me with certain needs, to Abu Abdullah (a.s.), and there was a green apple in front of him (a.s.), so I said to him (a.s.), ‘May I be sacrificed for you (a.s.)! What is this?’ So he (a.s.) said: ‘O Suleyman! I (a.s.) was slightly ill last night, so this was sent to me (a.s.). If I (a.s.) were to eat it, it would extinguish the heat and cool down the interior (stomach), and eliminate the fever’.

7. From him, from Abdullah Bin Hamaad and Yaqoub Bin Yazeed, from Al Qindy who said, ‘The people were struck by a plague and we were at Makkah, and I was struck by it, so I wrote to the Imam (a.s.), so he (a.s.) wrote back to me: ‘Eat the apple’. So I ate it, and I became healthy’.

عنه، عن أبى يوسف، عن القندى، قال: دخلت المدينة ومعى أخى سيف، فأصاب الناس الرعاف، وكان الرجل إذا رعف يومين مات فرجعت إلى المنزل، فإذا سيف أخى رعف رعافا شديدا، فدخلت على أبى عبد الله (ع) فقال: يا زياد أطعم سيفا التفاح، فرجعت فأطعمته إياه فبرئ.


8. From him, from Abu Yusuf, from Al Qindy who said, ‘I entered Al-Medina and with me was my brother Sayf. The people had been struck with nose bleed, and if a man had nose bleed for two days, he dies. So I returned to my house, and my brother Sayf had intense nose bleed. So I went to Abu Abdullah (a.s.), so he (a.s.) said: ‘Feed the apple to Sayf’. So I returned and fed it to him. He was freed (cured)’.
