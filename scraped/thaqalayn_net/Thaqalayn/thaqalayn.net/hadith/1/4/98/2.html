98. The Jinns Come to the Imam for Religious Instructions and for Dealing with their Affairs - Hadith #2

Back to chapter

2ـ عَلِيُّ بْنُ مُحَمَّدٍ عَنْ سَهْلِ بْنِ زِيَادٍ عَنْ عَلِيِّ بْنِ حَسَّانَ عَنْ إِبْرَاهِيمَ بْنِ إِسْمَاعِيلَ عَنِ ابْنِ جَبَلٍ عَنْ أَبِي عَبْدِ الله (عَلَيْهِ السَّلام) قَالَ كُنَّا بِبَابِهِ فَخَرَجَ عَلَيْنَا قَوْمٌ أَشْبَاهُ الزُّطِّ عَلَيْهِمْ أُزُرٌ وَأَكْسِيَةٌ فَسَأَلْنَا أَبَا عَبْدِ الله (عَلَيْهِ السَّلام) عَنْهُمْ فَقَالَ هَؤُلاءِ إِخْوَانُكُمْ مِنَ الْجِنِّ.


2. Ali ibn Muhammad has narrated from Sahl ibn Ziyad from Ali ibn Hassa’n from Ibrahim ibn ’Isma‘il from ibn Jabal who has said the following. “Once we were in front of the door of the house of abu ‘Abdallah (a.s.) that a people who looked Indian gypsies came out with loin clothe and covering on them. We asked abu ‘‘Abdallah (a.s.) about them and he said, “They were your brethren from Jinns.”


Grading:

Allamah Baqir al-Majlisi: ضعيف - Mir‘at al ‘Uqul Fi Sharh Akhbar Al al Rasul (4/292)

Shaykh Baqir al-Behbudi: ضعيف - Sahih al-Kafi

The gradings provided are observations of the chain of narrators, a hadith with a weak or unknown chain may still be correct, and a hadith with a reliable chain may still be incorrect.

Gradings may also differ depending on the methodology of the scholar, for example Allamah Baqir al-Majlisi's gradings would be more lenient compared to Shaykh Asif al-Mohseni's.
