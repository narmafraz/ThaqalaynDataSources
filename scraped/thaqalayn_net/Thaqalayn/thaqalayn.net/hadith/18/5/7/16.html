7. The analogist and the opinion - Hadith #16

Back to chapter

عنه، عن أبيه، عن النضر بن سويد، عن درست بن أبي منصور، عن محمد بن حكيم، قال: قلت لابي الحسن عليه السلام: إنا نتلاقى فيما بيننا فلا يكاد يرد علينا شئ إلا وعندنا فيه شئ وذلك شئ أنعم الله به علينا بكم، وقد يرد علينا الشئ وليس عندنا فيه شئ وعندنا ما يشبهه فنقيس على أحسنه؟ فقال: لا، وما لكم وللقياس، ثم قال: لعن الله أبا فلان، كان يقول: قال علي وقلت، وقالت الصحابة وقلت، ثم قال: كنت تجلس إليه؟ - قلت: لا ولكن هذا قوله، فقال أبو الحسن عليه السلام: إذا جاءكم ما تعلمون فقولوا، وإذا جائكم ما لا تعلمون فها (ووضع يده على فمه) فقلت: ولم ذاك؟ - قال: لان رسول الله صلى الله عليه وآله أتى الناس بما اكتفوا به على عهده وما يحتاجون إليه من بعده إلى يوم القيامة.


16. From him, from his father, from Al Nazar Bin Suweyd, from Darsat Bin Abu Mansour, from Muhammad Bin Hakeem who said, ‘I said to Abu Al-Hassan (a.s.), ‘We get together with regards to what is in between us, so whilst we are in the middle of that, most of the time when something is referred to us, but with us there is something with regards to it, and that is something which Allah (azwj) has Bestowed upon us by you (a.s.). And (sometimes) there is referred to us a matter, and there is not with us anything with regards to it, but with us there is something resembling it. Shall we analogise upon the best of it?’ The Imam (a.s.) said: ‘No. And what have you to do with the analogy?’ Then he (a.s.) said: ‘May Allah (azwj) Curse Abu so and so (Abu Haneefa). He (la) used to say, ‘Ali (a.s.) spoke, and I speak; and the companions spoke, and I speak’. Then he (a.s.) said: ‘You used to sit with him (la)?’ I said, ‘No, but this is his (la) speech’. So Abu Al-Hassan (a.s.) said: ‘Whenever there comes to you what you know about, so speak, and whenever there comes to you what you do not know about, so ha!’ and he (a.s.) placed his (a.s.) hand upon his (a.s.) mouth. So I said, ‘And why is that?’ He (a.s.) said: ‘Because Rasool-Allah (saww) gave to the people with what was sufficient for them in his (saww) era, and they are not needy from after him (saww) up to the Day of Judgement’.


Grading:

The gradings provided are observations of the chain of narrators, a hadith with a weak or unknown chain may still be correct, and a hadith with a reliable chain may still be incorrect.

Gradings may also differ depending on the methodology of the scholar, for example Allamah Baqir al-Majlisi's gradings would be more lenient compared to Shaykh Asif al-Mohseni's.
