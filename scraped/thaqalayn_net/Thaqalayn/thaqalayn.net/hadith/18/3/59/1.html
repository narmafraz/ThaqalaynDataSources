59. Punishment for the lie - Hadith #1

Back to chapter

عنه، عن عمر بن عثمان الخزاز، عن محمد بن سالم الكندى، عمن حدثه، عن أبي عبد الله عليه السلام، قال: كان علي عليه السلام عندكم إذا صعد المنبر يقول: ينبغى للمسلم أن يجتنب مؤاخاة الكذاب فانه لا يهنئك معه عيش، ينقل حديثك و ينقل الاحاديث اليك، كلما فنيت أحدوثة مطها بأخرى، حتى أنه ليحدث بالصدق فما يصدق، فينقل الاحاديث من بعض الناس إلى بعض، يكسب بينهم العداوة وينبت الشحناء في الصدور. وفي رواية أبي بصير، قال: سمعت أبا عبد الله عليه السلام يقول: ان العبد ليكذب حتى يكتب من الكذابين، فإذا كذب قال الله عزوجل: " كذب وفجر. ".


1. From him, from Umar Bin Usmaan Al Khazaaz, from Muhammad Bin Saalim Al Kindy, from the one who narrated it, (It has been narrated) from Abu Abdullah (a.s.) having said: ‘Ali (a.s.) was among you when he (a.s.) ascended the Pulpit saying: ‘It befits the Muslim that he should keep aside from the fraternity of the liar, for he does not come you in order to make your life comfortable. He transmits our (a.s.) Ahadeeth to you. Every time a Hadeeth fades away (as people tend to forget it) he comes up with another, to the extent even if he narrated with the truth (or not), to the (extent that) he is not believed. So he transmits the Ahadeeth from some of the people to the others (our (a.s.) enemies), attaining enmity between them and growing hatred in the chests’. And in a report of Abu Baseer who said, ‘I heard Abu Abdullah (a.s.) saying: ‘The servant lies until he is Written to be from the liars. So when he lies (again), Allah (azwj) Mighty and Majestic Says: “He has lied and transgressed”.


Grading:

The gradings provided are observations of the chain of narrators, a hadith with a weak or unknown chain may still be correct, and a hadith with a reliable chain may still be incorrect.

Gradings may also differ depending on the methodology of the scholar, for example Allamah Baqir al-Majlisi's gradings would be more lenient compared to Shaykh Asif al-Mohseni's.
