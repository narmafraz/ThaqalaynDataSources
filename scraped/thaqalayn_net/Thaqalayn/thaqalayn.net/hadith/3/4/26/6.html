26. Minimum and Maximum Tasbih in Ruku‘ and Sajdah - Hadith #6

Back to chapter

6ـ عَلِيُّ بْنُ مُحَمَّدٍ عَنْ بَعْضِ أَصْحَابِنَا عَنْ مَرْوَكِ بْنِ عُبَيْدٍ عَنْ بَعْضِ أَصْحَابِهِ عَنْ أَبِي جَعْفَرٍ (عَلَيْهِ الْسَّلام) قَالَ قُلْتُ لَهُ إِنِّي إِمَامُ مَسْجِدِ الْحَيِّ فَأَرْكَعُ بِهِمْ فَأَسْمَعُ خَفَقَانَ نِعَالِهِمْ وَأَنَا رَاكِعٌ فَقَالَ اصْبِرْ رُكُوعَكَ وَمِثْلَ رُكُوعِكَ فَإِنِ انْقَطَعَ وَإِلاَّ فَانْتَصِبْ قَائِماً.


6. Ali ibn Muhammad has narrated from certain persons of our people from Marwak ibn ‘Ubayd from certain persons of his people who has said the following: “I once said to abu Ja‘far, ‘Alayhi al-Salam, ‘I am an Imam of the village. During Salah (prayer) I hear the sound of their shoes walking when I am in Ruku‘ (bowing down on one’s knees).’ He (the Imam) said, ‘Wait in your Ruku‘ for the length of your Ruku‘ until the sound stops, otherwise, you can rise to stand up straight.’”


Grading:

Allamah Baqir al-Majlisi: مرسل - Mir‘at al ‘Uqul Fi Sharh Akhbar Al al Rasul (5/143)

The gradings provided are observations of the chain of narrators, a hadith with a weak or unknown chain may still be correct, and a hadith with a reliable chain may still be incorrect.

Gradings may also differ depending on the methodology of the scholar, for example Allamah Baqir al-Majlisi's gradings would be more lenient compared to Shaykh Asif al-Mohseni's.
