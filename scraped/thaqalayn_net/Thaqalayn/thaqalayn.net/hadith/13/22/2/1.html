2. When prayers are not blessed - Hadith #1

Back to chapter

2 - قال: حدثنا أبو بكر محمد بن عمر الجعابي قال: حدثنا أبو العباس أحمد بن محمد قال: حدثنا محمد بن عبد الله بن غالب قال: حدثنا الحسين بن علي بن رباح ، عن سيف بن عميرة قال: حدثنا محمد بن مروان قال: حدثنا عبد الله ابن أبي يعفور، عن أبي عبد الله جعفر بن محمد عليهما السلام قال: ثلاثة لا يقبل الله لهم صلاة: عبد آبق من مواليه حتى يرجع إليهم فيضع يده في أيديهم، ورجل أم قوما وهم له كارهون، وامرأة تبيت وزوجا عليها ساخط.


2. He said: Abu Bakr Muhammad ibn Umar al-Ji’abi reported to me from Abul Abbas Ahmad ibn Muhammad, who reported from Muhammad ibn Abdillah ibn Ghalib, who reported from al-Husayn ibn ‘Ali ibn Rabah, from Saif ibn Umairah, who reported from Muhammad ibn Marwan, who reported from Abdullah ibn Abi Ya’foor that: Abu Abdillah Ja’far ibn Muhammad, peace be upon him, said: "Allah does not bless the prayers with acceptance from three persons: From a slave who has escaped from his masters, till he returns and places his hand in their hands; from an Imam who leads people (in prayers) while they hate him; from a wife who passes a night while her husband is displeased with her."


Grading:

The gradings provided are observations of the chain of narrators, a hadith with a weak or unknown chain may still be correct, and a hadith with a reliable chain may still be incorrect.

Gradings may also differ depending on the methodology of the scholar, for example Allamah Baqir al-Majlisi's gradings would be more lenient compared to Shaykh Asif al-Mohseni's.
