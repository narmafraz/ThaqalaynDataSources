58. Such regarding what was in my possession from a copy of the book - Hadith #11

Back to chapter

عنه، عن السيارى، عن محمد بن جمهور العمى، عمن ذكره عن أبى - عبد الله (ع) قال، حرم من الذبيحة عشرة أشياء، وأحل من الميتة اثنتا عشرة شيئا، فأما ما يحرم من الذبيحة: فالدم، والفرث، والغدد، والطحال، والقضيب، والاثنيان، والرحم، والظلف، والقرن، والشعر، وأما ما يحل من الميتة: فالشعر، والصوف، والوبر، والناب، والقرن، والضرس، والظلف، والبيض، والانفحة، والظفر، والمخلب، والريش.


11. From him, from Al Sayari, from Muhammad Bin Jamhour Al A’ama, from the one who mentioned it, Abu Abdullah (a.s.) has said: ‘Ten things are Prohibited (to eat) from the slaughter, and twelve things are Permissible (to be used) from the dead. So, As for what is Prohibited from the slaughter – so it is the blood, and the dung, and the gland, and the spleen, and the penis, the intestine, and the womb, and the hoof, and the horn, and the hair’. And as for what is Permissible (to be used) from the dead – So it is the hair, and the wool, and fur, and the canines, and the horns, and the teeth, and the hoof, and the eggs, and the stomach lining, and the nail, and the claw, and feathers’.


Grading:

The gradings provided are observations of the chain of narrators, a hadith with a weak or unknown chain may still be correct, and a hadith with a reliable chain may still be incorrect.

Gradings may also differ depending on the methodology of the scholar, for example Allamah Baqir al-Majlisi's gradings would be more lenient compared to Shaykh Asif al-Mohseni's.
