5. The exclusive dining - Hadith #3

Back to chapter

عنه، عن عدة من أصحابنا، عن على بن أسباط، عن عمه يعقوب بن سالم، عن إسحاق بن عمار، عن الكاهلى، قال: سمعت أبا عبد الله (ع) يقول: إن يعقوب (ع) لما ذهب منه ابن يامين نادى يا رب يا رب أما ترحمني؟ - أذهبت عينى وأذهبت ابني؟ - فأوحى الله تبارك وتعالى لو أمتهما لاحييتهما حتى أجمع بينك وبينهما، ولكن أما تذكر الشاة التى ذبحتها وشويتها، وأكلت وفلان إلى جنبك صائم، لم تنله منها شيئا؟


3. Fromhim, from a number of our companions, from Ali Bin Asbaat, from his uncle Yaqoub Bin Salim, from Is’haq Bin Amaar, from Al Kahily who said, ‘I heard Abu Abdullah (a.s.) saying: ‘When he (as) went from him (as), Yaqoub (as) called out: ‘O Lord (azwj)! O Lord (azwj)! But, will You (azwj) not have Mercy upon me (as)? My eyesight has gone, and my (as) son (as) has gone?’ So, Allah (azwj) Blessed and High Revealed: “Even if you (as) two were to die, I (azwj) would Revive the both of you (as) until I (azwj) Gather you (as) and him (as). But, remember the sheep that was slaughtered and cooked and eaten, and so and so by your (as) side (neighbour) is Fasting, and he did not attain anything from it?” - قال ابن أسباط: قال: يعقوب: حدثنى الميثمى، عن أبى عبد الله (ع): أن يعقوب بعد ذلك كان ينادى مناديه كل غداة من منزله على فرسخ: " ألا من أراد الغداء فليأت آل يعقوب " وإذا أمسى نادى: " ألا من أراد العشاء فليأت آل يعقوب ". Ibn Asbat, from Yaqoub, from Al Maysami, Abu Abdullah (a.s.) has said: ‘Yaqoub (as), after that, got his (as) caller to call out every morning from his (as) house, to (a distance of) a Farsakh, ‘Indeed! The one who wants food should come to the Progeny of Yaqoub (as)!’ And when it was evening, called out, ‘Indeed! The one who intends the dinner, so he should come to the Progeny of Yaqoub (as)’.


Grading:

The gradings provided are observations of the chain of narrators, a hadith with a weak or unknown chain may still be correct, and a hadith with a reliable chain may still be incorrect.

Gradings may also differ depending on the methodology of the scholar, for example Allamah Baqir al-Majlisi's gradings would be more lenient compared to Shaykh Asif al-Mohseni's.
