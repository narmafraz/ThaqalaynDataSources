146. Taqsir (Trimming something from One’s Hair or Nails After Completing Sa‘y) of One Who Is for al-Hajj al-Tamattu‘ (advantageous) and Coming Out of the State of Ihram - Hadith #4

Back to chapter

4ـ عَلِيُّ بْنُ إِبْرَاهِيمَ عَنْ أَبِيهِ عَنِ ابْنِ أَبِي عُمَيْرٍ عَنْ جَمِيلِ بْنِ دَرَّاجٍ وَحَفْصِ بْنِ الْبَخْتَرِيِّ وَغَيْرِهِمَا عَنْ أَبِي عَبْدِ الله (عَلَيْهِ السَّلاَم) فِي مُحْرِمٍ يُقَصِّرُ مِنْ بَعْضٍ وَلا يُقَصِّرُ مِنْ بَعْضٍ قَالَ يُجْزِئُهُ.


4. Ali ibn Ibrahim has narrated from his father from ibn abu ‘Umayr from Jamil ibn Darraj and Flafs ibn al-Bakhtariy and other than these two who has said the following: “About a man who performs Taqsir (cutting some hair or finger nails and so on) of certain parts but not of other parts, he (the Imam) said, ‘It is sufficient for him.”


Grading:

Allamah Baqir al-Majlisi: حسنه الفضلء - Mir‘at al ‘Uqul Fi Sharh Akhbar Al al Rasul (8/80)

The gradings provided are observations of the chain of narrators, a hadith with a weak or unknown chain may still be correct, and a hadith with a reliable chain may still be incorrect.

Gradings may also differ depending on the methodology of the scholar, for example Allamah Baqir al-Majlisi's gradings would be more lenient compared to Shaykh Asif al-Mohseni's.
