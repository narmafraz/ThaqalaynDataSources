48. The Word in the Morning and Evening - Hadith #26

Back to chapter

26ـ وَفِي رِوَايَةِ سَعْدَانَ عَنْ أَبِي بَصِيرٍ عَنْ أَبِي عَبْدِ الله (عَلَيهِ السَّلام) مِثْلَهُ إِلا أَنَّهُ قَالَ أَهْوَنُهُ الْجُنُونُ وَالْجُذَامُ وَالْبَرَصُ وَإِنْ كَانَ شَقِيّاً رَجَوْتُ أَنْ يُحَوِّلَهُ الله عَزَّ وَجَلَّ إِلَى السَّعَادَةِ.


26. In the Hadith from Su’dan from abu Basir from abu ‘Abd Allah (a.s.) a similar Hadith is narrated except this: “. . . the least of which is leprosy and insanity and if he is listed among the unfortunate ones I can hope that Allah, the Most Majestic, the Most Holy, will change him to fortunate ones.”


Grading:

Allamah Baqir al-Majlisi: مرسل كالمجهول - Mir‘at al ‘Uqul Fi Sharh Akhbar Al al Rasul (2/277)

The gradings provided are observations of the chain of narrators, a hadith with a weak or unknown chain may still be correct, and a hadith with a reliable chain may still be incorrect.

Gradings may also differ depending on the methodology of the scholar, for example Allamah Baqir al-Majlisi's gradings would be more lenient compared to Shaykh Asif al-Mohseni's.
