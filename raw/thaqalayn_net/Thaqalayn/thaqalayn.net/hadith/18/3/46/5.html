46. Punishment of the adulterer - Hadith #5

Back to chapter

عنه، عن ابن أبي عمير، عن معاوية بن عمار، عن صباح بن سيابة، قال: كنت عند أبي عبد الله عليه السلام فقيل له: يزنى الزانى وهو مؤمن؟ - قال: إذا كان على بطنها سلب الايمان منه، فإذا قام رد عليه، قال: فانه إذا أراد أن يعود؟ - قال: ما أكثر ما يهم أن يعود ثم لا يعود. وفي رواية أبي عبيدة، عن أبي جعفر عليه السلام، قال: وجدنا في كتاب علي عليه السلام، قال: قال رسول الله (صلى الله عليه وآله) إذا كثر الزنا كثر موت الفجأة


5. From him, from Ibn Abu Umeyr, from Muawiya Bin Amaar, from Sabah Bin Sayabat who said, ‘I was in the presence of Abu Abdullah (a.s.), so it was said to him (a.s.), ‘The adulterer commits adultery and he is a Believer?’ He (a.s.) said: ‘When he was in the middle of it, the ‘Eman’ (faith) is Confiscated from him. So when he stands, it is returned back to him’. He said, ‘So if he intends to return (to committing adultery)?’ He (a.s.) said: ‘He gets concerned many a time if he wishes to return, then it (the faith) does not return’. And in a report of Abu Ubeyda, from Abu Ja’far (a.s.) having said: ‘We (a.s.) found in the Book of Ali (a.s.) that: ‘Rasool-Allah (saww) said: ‘When the adultery is rife, the sudden death would increase’.


Grading:

The gradings provided are observations of the chain of narrators, a hadith with a weak or unknown chain may still be correct, and a hadith with a reliable chain may still be incorrect.

Gradings may also differ depending on the methodology of the scholar, for example Allamah Baqir al-Majlisi's gradings would be more lenient compared to Shaykh Asif al-Mohseni's.
