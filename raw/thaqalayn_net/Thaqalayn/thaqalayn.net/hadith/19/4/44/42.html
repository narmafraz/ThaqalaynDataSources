44. The dates - Hadith #42

Back to chapter

عنه، عن ابن أبى نجران، عن محبوب بن يوسف، عن بعض أصحابنا، قال: لما قدم أبو عبد الله (ع) الحيرة خرج مع أصحاب لنا إلى بعض البساتين، فلما رآه صاحب - البستان أعظمه، فاجتنى له ألوانا من الرطب، فوضعه بين يديه، ووضع أبو عبد الله (ع) يده على لون منه، فقال: ما تسمون هذه؟ - فقلنا: " السابرى " قال: هذا نسميه عندنا " عذق بن زيد " ثم قال للون آخر، ما تسمون هذا؟ - (أو قال: فهذا؟ -) قلنا: الصرفان: قال: نعم التمر لا داء ولا غائلة، أما إنه من العجوزة.


42. From him, from Ibn Abu Najran, from Mahboub Bin Yusuf, form one of our companions who said, ‘When Abu Abdullah (a.s.) proceeded to Al-Hira, he (a.s.) went out with a companion of our to one of the gardens. So when the owner of the garden saw him (a.s.) paid his respects. So he bought out for him (a.s.) various types of the dates and placed them in front of him (a.s.), and Abu Abdullah (a.s.) placed his (a.s.) upon a type from it, so he (a.s.) said: ‘What do you call these?’ So we said, ‘Al-Sabiry’. The Imam (a.s.) said: ‘With us it is called Azaq Bin Zayd’. Then he (a.s.) said for another type: ‘What do you call these?’ (Or said: ‘For these?’). We said, ‘Al-Sarfaan’. He (a.s.) said: ‘The best of the dates. There is neither any disease in these nor anything unfavourable. But, it is from Al-Ajwazat’.


Grading:

The gradings provided are observations of the chain of narrators, a hadith with a weak or unknown chain may still be correct, and a hadith with a reliable chain may still be incorrect.

Gradings may also differ depending on the methodology of the scholar, for example Allamah Baqir al-Majlisi's gradings would be more lenient compared to Shaykh Asif al-Mohseni's.
