46. The Grapes - Hadith #3

Back to chapter

عنه، عن أبيه، عن ابن أبى عمير، عن هشام بن سالم، قال: كان على بن الحسين (ع) يعجبه العنب، فكان ذات يوم صائما فلما أفطر كان أول ما جاءت العنب، أتته أم ولد له بعنقود، فوضعته بين يديه، فجاء سائل فدفع إليه فدست إليه (أعنى إلى السائل) فاشترته ثم أتته، فوضعته بين يديه، فجاء سائل آخر فأعطاه، ففعلت أم الولد مثل ذلك حتى فعل ثلاث مرات، فلما كان في الرابع أكله.


3. From him, from his father, from Ibn Abu Umeyr, from Hisham Bin Saalim who said, ‘The grapes used to fascinate Ali (a.s.) Bin Al-Husayn (a.s.). So, one day, he (a.s.) was Fasting, so when he (a.s.) broke his (a.s.) Fast, the first thing which the mother of his (a.s.) son came with was a bunch of grapes. So she placed it in front of him (sws), and a beggar came up, so he (a.s.) (wanted it to be) handed over to him. So she gave it to him (meaning the beggar). So she bought (some more grapes) then came up with these and placed it in front of him (a.s.), and another beggar came up so he (a.s.) gave it to him. So the mother of the children did similar to that until she had done it three times. So when it was the fourth time, the Imam (a.s.) ate it’.


Grading:

The gradings provided are observations of the chain of narrators, a hadith with a weak or unknown chain may still be correct, and a hadith with a reliable chain may still be incorrect.

Gradings may also differ depending on the methodology of the scholar, for example Allamah Baqir al-Majlisi's gradings would be more lenient compared to Shaykh Asif al-Mohseni's.
