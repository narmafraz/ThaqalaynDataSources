2. The eggs

Back to book

- عنه، عن على بن الحكم، عن أبيه، عن سعد، عن الاصبغ، عن على (ع) قال: إن نبيا من الانبياء شكا إلى الله قلة النسل في أمته، فأمره أن يأمرهم بأكل البيض، ففعلوا، فكثر النسل فيهم.


1. From him, from Ali Bin Al Hakam, from his father, from Sa’ad, from Al Asbagh, It has been narrated from Ali (a.s.) that a Prophet (as) from the Prophets (as) complained to Allah (azwj) of the scarcity of the births in his community, so He (azwj) Commanded him (as) that he (as) should order them to eat the eggs. So they did it, and there was abundant births among them’.

2. 9. From him, from Muhammad Bin Isa, from his father, from his grandfather, and it is from Maysar Bin Abdul Aziz, Abu Abdullah (a.s.) having said: ‘The yolk of the eggs is light, and the whiteness (albumen) is heavy’.


عنه، عن يوسف بن السخت البصري، عن محمد بن جمهور، عن حمران بن أعين، قال: قلت لابي عبد الله (ع): إن أناسا يزعمون أن صفرة البيض أخف من البياض، فقال: إلى ما يذهبون في ذلك؟ - قلت: يزعمون أن الريش من البياض، وأن العظم والعصب من الصفرة، فقال أبو عبد الله (ع): فالريش أخفها


10. From him, from Yusuf Bin Al Sakht Al Basry, from Muhammad Bin Jamhour, from Hamran Bin Ayn who said, ‘I said to Abu Abdullah (a.s.), ‘The people are claiming that the yellow part (yolk) of the egg is the lightest part of the egg’. So he (a.s.) said: ‘To what are they going to with regards to that?’ I said, ‘They are claiming that the feathers are from the whiteness (albumen) and the bones and the nerves are from the yellow part (yolk)’. So he (a.s.) said: ‘But the feather is the lighter one of these’.
